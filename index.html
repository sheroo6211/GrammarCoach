Common Mistakes
The 20 most common English grammar mistakes — and exactly how to fix them
1. Using "I" incorrectly
WrongMe and John went to the store.
RightJohn and I went to the store.
💡 Remove the other person — "Me went to the store" sounds wrong, so use "I".
2. Your vs You're
WrongYour going to love this.
RightYou're going to love this.
💡 "You're" = "you are". If you can replace it with "you are", use you're.
3. There / Their / They're
WrongTheir going to there house.
RightThey're going to their house.
💡 There = place. Their = belonging. They're = they are.
4. Its vs It's
WrongThe dog wagged it's tail.
RightThe dog wagged its tail.
💡 "It's" = "it is". "Its" shows ownership. No apostrophe for possession.
5. Then vs Than
WrongShe is taller then me.
RightShe is taller than me.
💡 "Than" = comparison. "Then" = time sequence.
6. Affect vs Effect
WrongThe weather effected my mood.
RightThe weather affected my mood.
💡 "Affect" is a verb (action). "Effect" is a noun (result).
7. Could of / Should of
WrongI should of called you earlier.
RightI should have called you earlier.
💡 Always "could have / should have / would have" — never "of".
8. Loose vs Lose
WrongI always loose my keys.
RightI always lose my keys.
💡 "Lose" = to misplace something. "Loose" = not tight.
9. Apostrophes in plurals
WrongThe cat's are sleeping.
RightThe cats are sleeping.
💡 Never use an apostrophe to make a word plural.
10. Double negatives
WrongI don't know nothing about it.
RightI don't know anything about it.
💡 Two negatives cancel out and create a positive meaning.
11. Subject-verb agreement
WrongThe team are playing well.
RightThe team is playing well.
💡 A group noun takes a singular verb — "team", "staff", "group" are singular.
12. Unnecessary "very"
WeakShe was very tired.
StrongShe was exhausted.
💡 Instead of "very + adjective" use a single stronger word.
13. Starting with "Because"
WrongBecause I was late. I missed the bus.
RightBecause I was late, I missed the bus.
💡 "Because" clauses need to connect to a main sentence — don't leave them alone.
14. Fewer vs Less
WrongThere are less people here today.
RightThere are fewer people here today.
💡 "Fewer" for things you can count. "Less" for things you can't count.
15. Who vs Whom
WrongWhom is calling?
RightWho is calling?
💡 Use "who" as subject (doing the action). "Whom" as object (receiving the action).
16. Passive vs Active voice
PassiveThe report was written by me.
ActiveI wrote the report.
💡 Active voice is shorter, stronger, and clearer. Use it whenever possible.
17. Comma splice
WrongI love writing, it is my passion.
RightI love writing. It is my passion.
💡 Don't join two complete sentences with just a comma. Use a period or semicolon.
18. Redundant phrases
RedundantPlease revert back to me.
CleanPlease revert to me.
💡 "Revert" already means going back. Common redundancies: "end result", "past history", "future plans".
19. Mixing tenses
WrongShe walked in and sits down.
RightShe walked in and sat down.
💡 Keep the same tense throughout your sentence and paragraph.
20. "I" capitalization
WrongYesterday i went to the store.
RightYesterday I went to the store.
💡 "I" is always capitalized in English, no matter where it appears.
